Frequently Asked Questions

What happens when I call for help?

People you have added to your BFF list and other same-genedered users of the app nearby are notified of your request for help. Those responding to your call for help will be guided to your location via Google maps (integrated into the app). You may also track their approach via Google maps.

What happens when I track my location?

Your location is tracked and saved in our database. This data is available for 72 hours. You may review this via Past Requests menu option. If you choose to save the data (within 72 hours), this data is available to you till you delete the same. Otherwise the data will be deleted after 72 hours.

How do I track an unsafe route?

Under the maps tab, simply click on the tag icon. You can either mark a specific location as unsafe and flag the reason, or you can mark the entire route as unsafe. This can be shared with friends to let others know of unsafe areas.

How can I help others?

On the home screen of the app, tap the upward looking arrow at the bottom of the screen. It will allow you to "Join the Crowd" (for 90 minutes at a time). You will receive a notification (along with location and navigation info) when someone within a mile of your current location seeks assistance. You may "Leave the Crowd" anytime to stop receiving notifications.

Why don't I see all my Facebook friend?

Due to recent Facebook restrictions, you can add only those Facebook friends who have already installed the CrowdMe app to their mobile device. If you do not see the Facebook friends you want to add to your Safety Squad, invite them to download CrowdMe app. After they install the app, you will be able to add them to your Safety Squad.

Why do you delete all data after 72 hours?

The primary goal of the app is to ensure that no one in a vulnerable position is exploited. A person may be drunk or near drunk, but we want to encourage and enable such a person to seek help from others. We want people to use the app without having to worry about the volume of previous calls being held against them. Our aim is to promote a safe environment, free of sexual harassment and assault.

Revoked Consent

What is Consent?

Consent is freely given, enthusiastic, verbal affirmation. Consent can be revoked at anytime, and a lack of "no" does not imply consent.

Each button press is saved in order to record the moment the user "revoked consent." CrowdMe is trying to leverage technology, especially aritifical intelligence, in order to capture and convey consent while respecting the fluidity of consent.

Protecting Your Privacy

Here at CrowdMe, we take user privacy very seriously. Data is auto-deleted after 72 hours, but users have the option to save data indefinitely. The only exception to the auto-deletion are unsafe locationt tags. No user data is sold or shared in any form.

Flag Unsafe Locations

The aim is to crowd source unsafe locations and then utilize the data to help users avoid such tagged routes or locations. Tagging unsafe locations and routes creates a personal reminder about unsafe areas, as well as warns friends. Options for tagging include:

  • Single location or path
  • Poorly lit
  • Deserted
  • Attacked/Mugged

    Separation from Groups

    In the image, you can see three users, repersented by red, green, and purple location tags. All are attending a social event - could be a party.

    The three users may socialize without maintaining visual contact with each other. However, if one of them moves away from the other by say 300 yards or so, an auto-alert will happen to others in the group.

    • No need to maintain physical proximity or visual contact.
    • Even strangers can be part of an ad-hoc group.
    • Location based ad-hoc groups.

    In bars (and parties), one common assault pattern is to separate one person from a group of friends, take this person to a different location, etc. The aim is to leverage technology to prevent such assaults.

    Walking Buddy

    In the map, you can see three users, represented by green, red, and purple tags, walking to their destinations. Imaging this happening in the dark.

    The app can sense users nearby, indicate to users that they can form a walking group.

    • Walk to same desitnation.
    • Walk part of the way together.
    • Near train / bus stops, etc.

    Rather than walking alone, one can see if there are others nearby, who would also like to walk together. For example, if one takes a 6.00 PM subway back home everyday, one can even form a Walking buddy group.
