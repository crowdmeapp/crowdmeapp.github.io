Frequently Asked Questions

What happens when I call for help?

People you have added to your BFF list and other same-genedered users of the app nearby are notified of your request for help. Those responding to your call for help will be guided to your location via Google maps (integrated into the app). You may also track their approach via Google maps.

What happens when I track my location?

Your location is tracked and saved in our database. This data is available for 72 hours. You may review this via Past Requests menu option. If you choose to save the data (within 72 hours), this data is available to you till you delete the same. Otherwise the data will be deleted after 72 hours.

What happens to data related to my calls for help?

The data is retained for 72 hours from the time of initiation of the call for help. During the 72 hour window, you may access the data under the Past Requests menu option. You have the option to save the data. If you choose not to save (within 72 hours), the data will be deleted and lost for ever.

How can I help others?

On the home screen of the app, tap the upward looking arrow at the bottom of the screen. It will allow you to "Join the Crowd" (for 90 minutes at a time) - you will receive a notification (along with location and navigation info) when someone within a mile of your current location seeks assistance. You may "Leave the Crowd" anytime.

Why dont I see all my Facebook friend?

Due to recent Facebook restrictions, you can add only those Facebook friends who have already installed the CrowdMe app to their mobile device. If you do not see the Facebook friends you want to add to your Safety Squad, please ask them to install the CrowdMe app. After they install the app, you will be able to add them to your Safety Squad.

Why do you delete all data after 72 hours?

The primary goal of the app is to ensure that no one in a vulnerable position is exploited. A person may be drunk or near drunk and we want to encourage and enable such a person to seek help from others. In this context, we want to provide a platform that is judgement free. We want people to use the app without having to worry about "does this app count how many times I have gotten drunk in a week (month, year)..." Our aim is to promote a safe environment, free of sexual harassment and assault.
